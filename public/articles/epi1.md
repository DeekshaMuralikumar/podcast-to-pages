# You Outgrow People Without Hating Them.

Have you ever thought of someone you once couldn’t go a day without talking to and wondered when things changed? Not because of a fight. Not because anyone did something wrong. But because somewhere, the relationship stopped fitting the same way.

Welcome to another episode of Podcast to Pages. Here, we’re talking about outgrowing people — and why sometimes, there is no closure.

We treat people like forever contacts. When you choose a person as your best friend or your soulmate, you believe no one else could understand you the way they do. No one else could take care of you the way they do. You know each other’s inside jokes, the stories behind them, what they’re going through, and literally every little thing about them. You can’t even imagine a life without them in it. In your mind, you’ve already planned the next five years together.

At some point, you realize that the person who used to be a constant in your life just isn’t there the same way anymore. Not because of a fight or drama — just delayed communication and distance.     

I had a friend who was once anything and everything to me. We would text each other about the smallest things, send random memes at midnight, and somehow spend hours talking without running out of things to say. She knew every version of me, and I knew hers. Back then, I couldn’t imagine a life where she wasn’t a part of it.

But today, I don’t even know where she is or what she does. And that’s strange, isn’t it? How someone who once occupied so much of your life can slowly become someone you haven’t spoken to in months. Not because we stopped caring. Life just happened. Time changes people.

For a long time, I thought I had lost something. But maybe not every relationship is meant to last forever. Some people are meant to accompany us through a chapter, not the entire story. And that’s okay.

Because outgrowing people doesn’t mean you stopped loving them. It doesn’t erase the memories, the laughter, or the role they played in your life. It simply means you’ve accepted that people change, and so do relationships.

Not everyone who leaves becomes an enemy. Not every ending needs closure. Sometimes, you simply carry the memories, wish them well, and continue writing the next chapter of your life.

To you reading this, the people in your life today may or may not be part of your tomorrow. But your life is still yours. So love deeply, make memories, and live it to the fullest.
